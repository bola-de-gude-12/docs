As of Continia {param1} version 2024 R1, you may have the option to choose where you want your data stored. This only applies if your organization is a new Continia client though, and only under certain conditions described in this article. Continia currently has two centers for the storage of data – one in Europe (**North Europe**) and one in Australia (**AU East**). Which of these is used for the storage of your data depends on a number of factors, as outlined below.

If your organization is new to Continia, your data center storage depends on whether you use Microsoft Dynamics 365 [Business Central online](broken-reference) or Dynamics [NAV/Business Central on-premises](broken-reference).

## New Continia clients with Business Central online

If your organization uses Business Central online, your data center setup depends on the localization of your Base Application:

* If you use AU/NZ localization, you're automatically connected to the **AU East** data center. No configuration is required.
* If you use any other Microsoft-supported localization – such as DK, GB, or US – you're automatically connected to the **North Europe** data center. No configuration is required. For more information, see [Country/regional availability and supported languages](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics365/business-central/dev-itpro/compliance/apptest-countries-and-translations) (Microsoft article).
* If you use W1 localization, you're prompted to choose either **AU East** or **North Europe** during activation.

> \[!IMPORTANT] Once you've chosen your data center, you can’t switch to a different one.

## New Continia clients with NAV/Business Central on-premises

If your organization uses NAV/Business Central on-premises, you're prompted to choose either the **AU East** or the **North Europe** data center during activation.

> \[!IMPORTANT] Once you've chosen your data center, you can’t switch to a different one.
